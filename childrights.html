Fundamental Rights of Children

According to the United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Children all children are born with fundamental rights. The fundamental rights are approved and sanctioned in 1992 by the Indian constitution. All these rights are to ensure the safety and well being of the children and also to ensure equality. All the below mentioned rights are applicable to the children with any race, color, religion or language.

Some of the basic child rights are as follows.

 Right to Survival – to life, health, nutrition, name, nationality
 Right to Development – to education, care, leisure, recreation, cultural activities
 Right to Protection – from exploitation, abuse, neglect
 Right to Participation – to expression, information, thought, religion

Here are some of the rights which are specifically meant for the Children:

1. THE RIGHT TO AN IDENTITY (ARTICLES 7 AND 8)

Children are entitled to a name, legally registered with the government, that they belong to the country. To have a public identity and to have a record as the citizen’s of the country. To ensures national support and also access to social services.

2. THE RIGHT TO HEALTH (ARTICLES 23 AND 24)

Every child has the right for a good medical care under the article 23 and 24.Under this article they have given special support and care for the protection from harmful drugs and to have safe working environments which includes proper nutrition and safe drinking water.

3. THE RIGHT TO EDUCATION, (ARTICLE 28)

Under the right to education act every child has right to have free primary education in a safe environment which helps the children learn discipline and also helps them to learn to live a good life. Right amount of education shows them the right way to live their lives and prepares them for the future by having a good knowledge about how things work in the world.

4. THE RIGHT TO A FAMILY LIFE (ARTICLES 8, 9, 10, 16, 20, 22 AND 40)

Every child has the right to have a family which can take care of them and bring them up in a loving and caring environment. If by any chance any child has no family or they are given away by their biological parents then the child has the right to be taken care by caretakers. Every child has the right to live with their parents until it is harmful to them by any way.

5. THE RIGHT TO BE PROTECTED FROM VIOLENCE (ARTICLE 19 AND 34)

Every child has the right to be protected from violence, violence can be from family members or friends or at work place or by some stranger. Any child should not go through any kind of ill treatment or violence physically or sexually. Any child can’t be under the act of violence even by justifying as an act of discipline even by their parents. Any kind of violence to any child would result in a very serious legal action.

6. THE RIGHT TO AN OPINION (ARTICLE 12 AND 13)

All the children have a right to opinion, this again depends upon the maturity level they have. Right to opinion comes in to hand regarding their education or for some other matters if they like to put their opinion in public regarding some situation. They have the freedom of expression unless they are not harming others.

7. THE RIGHT TO BE PROTECTED FROM ARMED CONFLICT (ARTICLES 38 AND 39)

Every child has a right to be protected and kept in safe environment in the time of war. They must be protected by the armed conflicts which may turn them into refugees or prisoners or lead them into any wrong direction by which they can be harmful to others. The government should also ensure that the children are not being any part of armed struggle.

8. THE RIGHT TO BE PROTECTED FROM EXPLOITATION (ARTICLES 19, 32, 34, 36 AND 39)

Exploitation means the action or fact of treating someone unfairly in order to benefit from their work and generally it is achieved trough violence or by tricking. This act is to protect the children to work in harmful environment or be victim’s to any kind of abuse even by their parents even if it is justified for achieving discipline. Sexual exploitation, another dimension of exploitation, is also prohibited, as an activity that takes advantage of them. Children cannot be punished cruelly even if it is under the justice. Death or life sentence are not permitted including sentences with prisoners aren’t permitted legally.

Some other fundamental rights that children have as any other adult:

 Right to equality (Article 14)
 Right against discrimination (Article 15)
 Right to personal liberty and due process of law (Article 21)
 Right to being protected from being trafficked and forced into bonded labor (Article 23)
 Right of minorities for protection of their interests (Article 29)
 Right of weaker sections of the people to be protected from social injustice and all forms of exploitation (Article 46)
 Right to nutrition and standard of living and improved public health (Article 47)
